## MEMORANDUM

**TO:** OSAA State Championship Committee
**FROM:** Oregon Tennis Rankings Project
**DATE:** March 16, 2026
**RE:** Roster Size Analysis — Viability of Team Dual Match Format for 4A/3A/2A/1A Tennis

---

### Summary

A concern has been raised that many smaller schools lack sufficient roster depth to participate in a team dual match format, which requires a minimum of six (6) players. An analysis of the 2025 season match data from TennisReporting — covering all 4A/3A/2A/1A programs in Oregon — shows that **this concern applies to only 4 boys programs and 2 girls programs statewide.**

---

### Findings

**Boys (4A/3A/2A/1A): 38 of 42 active programs (90%) fielded 6 or more players**

| School            | Classification | District             | Players |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------------|---------|
| Arlington         | 1A             | Special District 4   | 3       |
| Weston-McEwen     | 2A             | Special District 4   | 2       |
| Condon            | 1A             | Special District 4   | 4       |
| Sherman           | 1A             | Special District 4   | 4       |

**Girls (4A/3A/2A/1A): 48 of 50 active programs (96%) fielded 6 or more players**

| School            | Classification | District             | Players |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------------|---------|
| Arlington         | 1A             | Special District 4   | 5       |
| Condon            | 1A             | Special District 4   | 2       |

All six programs are 1A or 2A schools located in Special District 4 (eastern Oregon). No school in Special Districts 1, 2, 3, or 5 lacked sufficient players to compete in a team format.

---

### Context

- Player counts are based on unique athletes who appeared in at least one recorded match during the 2025 season.
- Schools that did not field a team in 2025 (e.g., Sutherlin boys, Pleasant Hill boys) are excluded from the active program count.
- Two additional boys programs — Molalla (5 players) and Junction City (5 players) — fell one player short but may have had additional rostered athletes who did not appear in match records.
- Several schools listed with 0 players in the data either did not field a team or competed exclusively in tri-meet/tournament formats not captured in dual match reporting.

### Conclusion

The data does not support the claim that a team dual match format would be broadly unworkable for smaller schools. Of the 92 active 4A/3A/2A/1A programs across boys and girls tennis in 2025, **86 (93%) had at least 6 players** — the minimum needed for a team event. The roster depth concern is limited to a small number of 1A programs concentrated in a single district. Accommodations for these programs (e.g., default/forfeit provisions for unfilled flights) could address the issue without preventing the remaining programs from competing in a team format.
