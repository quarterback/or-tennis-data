Fair Bracket Generator — OSAA-style individual tennis

A demonstration that the individual state draw can be seeded fairly within OSAA's existing rules. Byes are allocated strictly by merit — seeds → district champions → 2nd → 3rd → 4th — so unseeded 3rd/4th finishers land on play-in (pigtail) lines rather than being handed a bye over a district champion or a 2nd-place finisher. It honors every separation rule (same district not in round 1; same school not before the semifinals; a school's district 1&2 not before the final) and works for any number of districts (4–8+) and qualifiers-per-district. The scoring panel is illustrative only — a hypothetical chalk run, not any real tournament — showing how the legacy round-reached points (where a bye buys points) compare to a per-win scheme.

Team points — chalk run (higher merit wins)

Round-based (legacy OSAA): 2 per main-draw win, a first-round bye worth +2 if the second-round match is won, +1 for the 3rd-place match. Per-win: 1 per main-draw win + a 4/3/2/1 podium bonus; a bye is worth 0. Both add the consolation bracket (capped at 2): +0.5 to reach its semifinal, +0.5 the final, +1 to win it.
