2027 OSAA Mixed Doubles — Standby List Tool

In the proposed 2027 format a 16-line mixed doubles bracket replaces consolation in each classification. Pairs are same-school and only one partner must be a state qualifier; the other can be a rostered teammate who played the regular season and districts but didn't qualify (+roster). This tool demonstrates the part that's new to OSAA: the public standby list that fills the provisional slots when qualifiers lose their first match. All student names are fictional; schools and special districts are real.
How mixed doubles fills. The bracket is set with the known main draw first — 10–14 automatic district bids submitted the Saturday after districts. The remaining 2–8 provisional slots fill continuously off this standby list as Round 1 plays out, exactly like a consolation bracket fills as losers become known. The list publishes ranked by Tier 1 (pigtail/R32 losers) ahead of Tier 2 (R16 losers), then weaker district finish first. When a qualifier loses R1 its pair becomes eligible and, if a slot is open, clears straight into the bracket; when a qualifier wins, its pair drops out (still alive in singles/doubles). Once the slots are full, later-eligible pairs wait as alternates. If the pool runs dry, unfilled bracket lines simply play as byes. Use Step R1 to watch results post one at a time.

Dual team finals (2027 new event)

Each class's dual tournament ends with a Boys final and a Girls final at the state site — an 8-match dual (4 singles + 4 doubles), play-to-clinch: first school to 5 wins takes the title; remaining matches are dead rubbers.
